Personal Digital Data

For one week, I logged everything I listened to. I took note, of the application I used, how long I listened, where I listened, and what I was doing while listening. After recording all the data for that week, I turned it into a visualization. .

By studying my habits for a week, I got a feeling for how much time I actually spent on the three apps that I used, which were YouTube, Spotify, and Podcasts. To see all of my time, which is LITERALLY MY LIFE, measured, categorized, and visualized into litlle colorful bubbles, gave me a little more perspective on how much time there really is in a day or week. I also learned about how my enjoyment of an app affects how long I listen to it. Some content made it easy to listen for hours at a time, while others were better suited for a few minutes of my time.

The difference in the time that I spent on each application led me to investigate each of the apps, so that I could learn the reasoning and cause behind my time usage. In the next section, I explain what I learned about why I was spreading my time differently between the apps and why I sometimes listened for much longer than others.
